My definition of innovation includes tech, entrepreneurship, design, and business development. Here is a list of resources that are at or affiliated with medical schools:

+ [Duke - DUSOMIE](http://sites.duke.edu/dusomie/)
+ [Yale - Center for Biomedical and Interventional Technology (CBIT)](http://medicine.yale.edu/cbit/)
+ [Johns Hopkins - FastForward](http://engineering.jhu.edu/fastforward/)
+ [Penn Medicine - Center for Health Care Innovation](http://www.uphs.upenn.edu/center-for-innovation/)
+ [Emory, Georgia Tech - Forge](http://forgehealth.org)
+ [Harvard University - Harvard Innovation Lab](https://i-lab.harvard.edu/)
+ [Harvard University - Blavatnik Biomedical Accelerator](http://otd.harvard.edu/accelerators/blavatnik-biomedical-accelerator/)
+ [Brigham & Women's Hopsital - Innovation Hub](http://disruptingmedicine.org/)
+ [Harvard Business School - Rock Accelerator](http://www.hbs.edu/entrepreneurship/mbacurriculum/rock-accelerator.html)
+ [MIT - Hacking Medicine](http://hackingmedicine.mit.edu/)
+ [Stanford - StartX](startx.stanford.edu)
+ [Mayo Clinic - Center for Innovation](http://www.mayo.edu/center-for-innovation/)
+ [Tufts - MedStart (Hackathon)](http://tuftsmedstart.com/)
